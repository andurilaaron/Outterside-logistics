Customs Clearance Support

Clearance support built around clean cargo data, accurate documentation, broker coordination and practical exception management.

Documentation before clearance

Clearance starts before the goods arrive.

Customs problems are usually created upstream: weak descriptions, missing values, unclear country of origin, mismatched weights, incomplete packing details or vague broker instructions.

Outterside Logistics helps build a clean shipment data pack so your broker, forwarder or receiver has what they need before the shipment reaches the border.

Support areas

  • Commercial invoice data preparation
  • Packing list data preparation
  • Goods description review
  • Country of origin and valuation data capture
  • Broker instruction support
  • Clearance exception follow-up
  • Import/export document pack structure

What we check before clearance

Descriptions

Goods descriptions need to be specific enough for customs, brokers and carriers to understand what is moving.

Values

Invoice values, currency and transaction detail need to align with the shipment purpose and supporting records.

Origin

Country of origin data should be captured clearly to support clearance, duty assessment and broker review.

Weights

Gross weight, net weight and package-level detail need to match freight and customs documents.

Parties

Shipper, consignee, importer, exporter and notify parties need to be clear before the shipment is lodged.

Broker Handover

Instructions should remove ambiguity around shipment purpose, documents, timing and clearance pathway.
