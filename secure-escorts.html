Secure Escort Services

Escort and observer support for classified, sensitive, high-value or controlled freight where normal transport visibility is not enough.

Classified and sensitive cargo

Extra control around the movement, not unnecessary theatre.

Secure escort services are used where loss, exposure, route deviation, unauthorised stops, uncontrolled handover or poor visibility would create unacceptable risk.

Outterside Logistics can support escort planning, second-person observer services, route discipline, dispatch and arrival attendance, exception reporting, and movement close-out documentation.

Escort role can include

  • Second-person observer support
  • Route compliance monitoring
  • Dispatch and arrival attendance
  • Visual continuity where practical
  • Exception reporting
  • Seal/load condition observations where directed
  • Chain-of-custody support records

Use cases

Classified Cargo

Movements requiring extra personnel, documented handover, route control or client security procedures.

High-Value Equipment

Freight where exposure, loss or delay creates commercial or operational risk.

Defence-Related Goods

Support for sensitive goods where lawful authority, site access and client instructions need discipline.

Restricted Sites

Movements into controlled facilities where access windows, contact points and arrival processes matter.

Urgent Movements

Short-notice movements requiring rapid mobilisation and clear escalation pathways.

Exception Management

Clear reporting for delays, route deviations, unauthorised stops, damage or access issues.

Secure movement workflow

1

Risk and requirement review

Confirm cargo sensitivity, route, timing, access authority, insurance, escalation contacts and site rules.

2

Escort plan

Define escort role, vehicle arrangement, communication rhythm, stop controls, exception triggers and handover evidence.

3

Movement execution

Conduct dispatch, transit and arrival support in accordance with agreed instructions.

4

Report close-out

Return movement notes, delivery evidence, exception report and any incident documentation.
